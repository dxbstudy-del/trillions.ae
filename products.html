Product focus

Product categories Trillions can help source and compare.

Trillions supports selected categories where supplier quality, specification clarity, packaging, documents, and GCC market fit can change the outcome of a sourcing decision.

Not a fixed catalog Share the product you want, and Trillions will review whether supplier sourcing, quotation comparison, private label, or import coordination is the right next step.

Categories

Focus areas with sourcing and private-label potential.

These categories are practical starting points. Trillions can also review custom product opportunities when there is enough information to evaluate supplier capability, documents, packaging, and logistics.

01

Outdoor and camping

Camping accessories, portable outdoor equipment, vehicle camping add-ons, storage, lighting, tents, shade products, and private-label outdoor concepts.

What matters:
  • Material durability, heat resistance, packing size, and warranty expectations.
  • Sample quality, accessories, replacement parts, and packaging for GCC buyers.
02

RC and hobby products

Remote-control vehicles, hobby accessories, batteries, spare parts, upgrades, custom bundles, and market-ready kit concepts.

What matters:
  • Technical specifications, battery details, controller type, spare parts, and warranty.
  • Supplier capability for customization, packaging, manuals, and after-sales support.
03

Home and lifestyle

Home accessories, diffusers, organization products, decor, wellness-adjacent items, lifestyle gifts, and retail packaging concepts.

What matters:
  • Material finish, packaging quality, scent or ingredient details, and product safety documents.
  • Visual differentiation, Arabic/English presentation, and retail shelf readiness.
04

Tools and machinery

Commercial equipment, workshop tools, construction support items, rental-product opportunities, machine accessories, and supplier comparisons.

What matters:
  • Technical data, power requirements, spare parts, operating manuals, and warranty terms.
  • Shipping weight, packing method, certificates, and serviceability in the GCC.
05

Fashion accessories

Bags, accessories, display materials, packaging, gift sets, custom branding, and private-label presentation for retail concepts.

What matters:
  • Material, stitching, hardware, color options, MOQ, logo method, and packaging.
  • Sample review, finish consistency, photo readiness, and retail presentation.
06

Custom opportunities

New categories can be reviewed case by case when there is enough buyer demand, supplier information, specification detail, or product evidence.

What matters:
  • Clear product use case, target buyer, quantity, target price, and destination market.
  • Supplier capability, documentation, logistics practicality, and market fit.

What to provide

Send enough detail for a useful sourcing review.

The better the starting information, the faster Trillions can compare suppliers, identify missing details, and prepare the right follow-up questions.

Product evidencePhotos, catalog page, website link, model number, sample notes, or competitor example.
Buying targetQuantity, budget or target price, destination country/city, and expected order timing.
Supplier materialSupplier name, quotation, chat screenshots, certificates, product files, or payment terms.
Brand or market needsPackaging direction, logo needs, Arabic/English labels, warranty expectations, and target customer.

How categories are reviewed

Trillions looks beyond whether a supplier can quote.

Supplier capabilityDoes the supplier understand the product, customization, MOQ, samples, and production expectations?
Specification clarityAre material, dimensions, accessories, packaging, warranty, and documents clear enough to compare?
GCC readinessWill the product, labels, climate suitability, support expectations, and logistics make sense for the destination market?
