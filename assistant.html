What to include

A stronger sourcing inquiry starts with clear inputs.

Product evidencePhotos, catalog page, model number, supplier link, or competitor example.
Buying targetQuantity, destination country or city, target price, and expected order timing.
Supplier materialQuotation, supplier name, MOQ, payment terms, lead time, warranty, and certificates.
Market needsPackaging, logo, local-language labels, after-sales expectations, and destination-market readiness.

What the assistant can and cannot do

Useful for preparation, not a replacement for verified sourcing work.

Can help with

  • Drafting a sourcing inquiry or supplier follow-up message.
  • Listing missing details in a quotation or product brief.
  • Preparing private-label, packaging, and sample questions.
  • Organizing a buyer brief for destination-market review.

Cannot do

  • Guarantee live prices, supplier availability, or delivery timelines.
  • Verify a factory, license, certificate, or product claim by itself.
  • Provide legal, customs, tax, safety, or compliance approval.
  • Replace formal quotation review, sample checks, or import coordination.
