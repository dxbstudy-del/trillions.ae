No customs approval guaranteeTrillions reviews available information and questions. Customs acceptance, regulatory approval, HS codes, duties, and compliance require separate confirmation.

Review areas

Import readiness starts with clean supplier documents.

Packing details

Carton quantity, units per carton, gross weight, net weight, carton dimensions, pallet details, and packing method.

Invoice and packing list review

Supplier name, buyer details, item descriptions, quantities, values, currency, incoterms, and consistency between documents.

Certificate questions

Certificate type, issuing body, product match, validity, supplier name, test report references, and market relevance.

HS-code questions

Potential classification questions to discuss with qualified customs brokers or specialists. Trillions does not assign final HS codes.

Shipping terms

FOB, EXW, CIF, delivery assumptions, port names, freight handoff, insurance, and who handles export documents.

Labeling and regulated products

Arabic/English labels, warnings, manuals, product safety, restricted goods, batteries, cosmetics, electrical items, and other regulated-product concerns.

Before shipment

Review documents before the order becomes urgent.

Import readiness review helps identify missing information, but it does not replace customs, legal, tax, or regulatory advice.
